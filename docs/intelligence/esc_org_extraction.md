## 3. Strategic Intelligence Extraction

### Role in Ecosystem
**Certification authority** ‚Äî not an operator, marketplace, or platform. ESC trains and certifies escorts. They do NOT:
- Match escorts to loads
- Track escort performance
- Provide real-time data
- Manage compliance across states
- Offer equipment marketplace
- Handle dispatch or scheduling

### Revenue Model
- **Training fees:** $265 (PEVO), $325 (WITPAC), $2,200+ (private team)
- **Recertification:** 3-year cycle drives recurring revenue ($100 discount for renewals)
- **No marketplace margin, no data fees, no subscriptions**
- **Estimated revenue:** Low millions ‚Äî training-only is a ceiling

### Geography
- **Primary:** Washington State (WA)
- **Reciprocity:** Multi-state acceptance of WA PEVO card (state-specific, documented)
- **Canada:** WITPAC mentions Canadian CDL equivalency (Class A)
- **Limitation:** Not a national certification body ‚Äî state-by-state acceptance varies

### Corridors Touched
- Pacific NW energy corridors (WA, OR, ID)
- Wind industry corridors (WITPAC-specific ‚Äî national)
- Cross-border US/CA (WITPAC CDL Canadian equivalency)

### Compliance Intelligence
- WA State PEVO regulations: WAC 468-38-100
- Federal MUTCD Part 6 (flagging guidelines)
- USDOT state regulations reference (FHWA)
- WITPAC prerequisites: CDL or PEVO certification
- 3-year certification validity cycle
- Age requirement: 18+
- Valid driver's license required

### Monetization Hooks (for HCOS)
1. **Certification verification API** ‚Äî they have manual lookup, we automate it
2. **Training referral affiliate** ‚Äî route new escorts to ESC, earn referral
3. **Reciprocity tracker** ‚Äî multi-state acceptance data (they have articles, we have a database)
4. **Equipment compliance bundles** ‚Äî they recommend gear, we sell it
5. **Performance scoring post-certification** ‚Äî they certify, we score

### Data Signals
