## The Wedge Product: AI Permit + Route Decision Engine

```
Carrier enters dimensions

                -> Cost estimate
```

## The 4-Phase Stack Expansion

### Phase A: Permit Intelligence Platform (Year 0-2)
**Goal**: Become the fastest permit engine in the industry.

**AI Stack - Layer 1: Regulatory Intelligence Engine**
- Centralized permit storage (all 50 states)
- Renewal reminders and compliance calendar
- Real-time application tracking
- Integration with state/federal rule databases

**AI Stack - Layer 2: Predictive Routing Core**
- Equipment attribute analysis
- Load characteristic modeling
- Jurisdiction rule engine
- Route restriction database
- ML-based route optimization (the moat)

**Revenue**:
- Permit automation fees (per-transaction)
- SaaS subscriptions (monthly/annual)
- Enterprise dashboards (premium tier)

---

### Phase B: Compliance Cloud (Year 3-5)
**Goal**: Category creation. You are now a platform.

**Why**:
- Compliance is shifting to CONTINUOUS MONITORING, not episodic audits.
- Legacy permit shops are transactional. You are operational.
- Smaller fleets (your ideal early customer) face financial risk when behind on compliance tech.

**Revenue**:
- Continuous compliance monitoring subscriptions
- Regulatory update intelligence feeds
- Audit defense and documentation services

---

### Phase C: Logistics Operating System (Year 5-8)
**Goal**: Embed into freight workflows. Switching costs explode.

**Why**:
- Global logistics software may exceed $37B by 2033.
- You don't compete with permit companies anymore.
- You compete with INFRASTRUCTURE SOFTWARE.

**Revenue**:
- Full TMS integration fees
- Dispatch orchestration
- Fleet management dashboards

---

### Phase D: Data Monopoly Layer (Year 8-10)
**Goal**: Competitors cannot replicate your dataset. That is the moat.

**When every permit + route flows through you, you own**:
- Clearance intelligence (bridge/road/weight data)
- Enforcement patterns (where violations happen)
- Risk models (predictive compliance scoring)
- Pricing intelligence (what loads cost to move, where)

This is the "Google of Heavy Transport" — not hyperbole.
