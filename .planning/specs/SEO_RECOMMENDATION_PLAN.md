# Haul Command SEO Recommendation Plan
Built around the operating law: upgrade only, no dead ends, role + intent as SEO logic, money-left-on-the-table extraction every sprint, Google/local/AI-search truth, and 120-country expansion from day one.

## WAVE 1 — DAY-1 AUTHORITY FOUNDATION

**01. HC-W1-01 — Intent Graph + Canonical Page Family Map**
Build one canonical architecture across directory, profiles, roles, services, loads, corridors, rates, tools, regulations, glossary, locations, claims, sponsor surfaces, and data products. Every important page gets a parent, siblings, related pages, and one dominant next action. This is the center of your own role+intent and no-dead-end law, and it also matches Google’s requirement that important pages be found through crawlable HTML links, not fragile JS-only paths.

**02. HC-W1-02 — Crawlable Navigation + Breadcrumb Rail**
Make every core navigation path crawlable with real `<a href>` links. Put breadcrumb rails on all page families and make them reflect the typical user path, not just the URL string. This strengthens crawl depth, orientation, and snippet eligibility.

**03. HC-W1-03 — Canonical URL / Slug / Hreflang Discipline**
Enforce one canonical URL pattern for every page family, kill weak duplicate route variants, normalize hyphenation, and wire hreflang from one canonical truth source. Your advisor dump already shows `v_hreflang_sets` and `slug_audit_log` as important supporting objects, so treat this as foundation, not polish.

**04. HC-W1-04 — Answer Block + Structured Data Contract**
Every page that answers a real question needs a visible quick-answer block, expanded answer, freshness state, confidence state, source path, and CTA. Then add matching JSON-LD only for what is visible on the page. Google’s current guidance is explicit: structured data should be accurate, up to date, and must not describe content users cannot actually see. Google also recommends JSON-LD as the easiest format to maintain at scale.

**05. HC-W1-05 — SEO Data Source Hardening**
Before you scale more public pages, harden the SEO-facing data sources already flagged in your advisor dump, especially `directory_active_loads_public`, `v_hot_corridor_loads`, `v_hreflang_sets`, `v_page_cannibalization_score`, `v_public_corridor_pages`, `hc_corridor_public_v1`, `v_city_market_pulse`, `load_intelligence`, `v_leaderboard_latest`, `v_adgrid_live_inventory`, `v_claim_pipeline_roi`, and `claim_kpi_summary`. Also harden the supporting SEO tables `seo_schema_templates`, `seo_scorecards`, `seo_taxonomies`, `slug_audit_log`, and `social_content_queue`. If these are shaky, the SEO layer becomes shaky.

**06. HC-W1-06 — Internal Link Engine: Glossary ↔ Regulations ↔ Tools ↔ Roles ↔ Locations ↔ Corridors**
Make internal linking a system, not hand-editing. Every glossary page should feed regulation pages, tool pages, role pages, and location pages. Every regulation page should feed related glossary terms, tools, and service pages. Every corridor page should feed nearby cities, related services, restrictions, and live supply surfaces. This directly matches your own topical-authority and cluster logic.

**07. HC-W1-07 — Claim-First Commercial CTA Layer**
Every authority page should push one of these next moves: claim listing, request availability, book support, check rules, use tool, install app, or sponsor market. Informational traffic without a claim or commercial bridge leaves money on the table, and your own operating law explicitly forbids that.

**08. HC-W1-08 — Local Truth + GBP Readiness System**
Build city/state/service-area logic around real service areas, real contact points, real location truth, real hours, and real staffed locations only. For profiles and local conquest pages, never fake storefronts or virtual offices. Google’s Business Profile guidance is very clear here, especially for service-area businesses.

**09. HC-W1-09 — Search Console / Sitemap / Rich Result QA Stack**
Ship XML sitemap segmentation by page family, canonical-only submission, and a weekly QA routine for schema errors, breadcrumbs, index coverage, and crawl anomalies. If you publish dataset-style pages, mark up the canonical landing pages and use `sameAs` carefully where the same dataset appears in multiple places.

**10. HC-W1-10 — Trust / Review / Report-Card Rules**
Use report cards, proof, freshness, and verification as trust weapons, but do not fake review markup. Google does not allow self-serving review stars on your own local business or organization pages, so Haul Command should lean into proof surfaces and report cards instead of trying to juice stars the wrong way.

## WAVE 2 — DENSITY, CLUSTERS, AND CONVERSION EXPANSION

**11. HC-W2-01 — City / State / Corridor Cluster Expansion**
For every live market, build a cluster that ties city pages, nearby city pages, corridor pages, regulations, glossary, tools, and commercial service pages into one loop. This is how you stop being “a page” and become the market’s reference layer. It also fits your hyperlocal dominance law.

**12. HC-W2-02 — Role × Intent × Market Landing Grid**
Create landing pages for real buyer intent, not generic categories. Examples: pilot car near me, escort requirements by state, heavy haul route survey by corridor, oversize escort rates by market, urgent replacement escort, broker capacity by lane, and permit-help pages by region. Your own prompt says role + intent + market context should govern product and SEO logic, so this is mandatory, not optional.

**13. HC-W2-03 — Regulation-to-Tool-to-Service Money Paths**
Every rule page should point to the tool that solves the problem and the service page that monetizes the problem. Every tool page should point back to the rule, the glossary explanation, and the provider/service surface. This turns informational SEO into a conversion loop instead of an isolated article.

**14. HC-W2-04 — Available-Now / Repositioning / Backhaul SEO Surfaces**
Publish real-time-feeling but truthful surfaces for available now, repositioning, backhaul, and coverage gap demand. Your prompt explicitly lists these as competitor-kill surfaces, and they are also high-intent pages that can win long-tail search while feeding booking, claim, and app install.

**15. HC-W2-05 — Comparison, Alternative, and Decision Pages**
Build pages like Haul Command vs [competitor], best pilot car software, best escort load board, pilot car near me vs directory search, and what to do when you can’t find an escort in [market]. Google’s people-first guidance rewards original analysis and genuinely useful comparisons, not generic fluff.

**16. HC-W2-06 — AdGrid Inventory Layer on High-Intent Pages**
Monetize the pages with the highest purchase intent first: city pages, corridor pages, urgent replacement pages, regulations pages, tool pages, and claim pages. Your own operating law says AdGrid must be impossible to overlook, and these are the surfaces where sponsor inventory actually feels native instead of forced.

**17. HC-W2-07 — Dataset Teasers + Corridor Intelligence Pages**
Turn market data into public teaser pages and enterprise-ready dataset pages. Google supports dataset markup on canonical landing pages, and this gives you both search visibility and a clean entry point into data products.

**18. HC-W2-08 — Social Gravity + Market Pulse Pages**
Use truthful activity layers like hazard reports, market pulse, recent observations, and fresh market signals to make local pages feel alive. Your prompt wants sticky utility-first social gravity, not dead feeds, and your advisor data already shows relevant objects like `v_city_market_pulse` and `behavioral_events` that can support this once hardened.

**19. HC-W2-09 — Training / Academy / Forms Hub Cluster**
Treat training, certification, forms, and compliance templates as a major SEO and monetization family. These pages attract high-intent users early in their journey, then feed them into glossary, rules, service, and marketplace flows. This is one of the clearest “money left on the table” areas in the heavy haul niche.

## WAVE 3 — MONOPOLY MACHINE

**20. HC-W3-01 — 120-Country Expansion Engine**
Roll out the same page-family logic globally: country → region/state/province → city → corridor → glossary/regulation/tool/service/claim. Your own master prompt makes 120-country scope canonical, so the SEO system should be built once and expanded many times, not rebuilt country by country.

**21. HC-W3-02 — Official Source Map + Authority Layer**
For every country and submarket, publish official source maps, freshness states, confidence states, and what-to-do-next guidance. Google’s people-first posture rewards reliable, well-sourced, useful content, and this becomes a moat because most competitors stop at shallow summaries.

**22. HC-W3-03 — AI-Search Answer Surface System**
Build every major page so it can be quoted, summarized, or cited cleanly by AI search systems: concise answer, expanded explanation, visible proof, freshness, confidence, source map, and next move. This is already written into your prompt, and it also fits Google’s direction toward clearer machine-readable meaning and structured understanding.

**23. HC-W3-04 — Infrastructure / Partner / Property Network Pages**
Build pages for yards, staging, hotels, installers, route support locations, secure parking, equipment, and partner services. These pages expand topical authority, create local pack opportunities, and open affiliate / sponsor / marketplace revenue at the same time.

**24. HC-W3-05 — Enterprise Data Products + API Entry System**
Every internal analytics asset should become either a teaser page, a pro feature, an enterprise export, or an API landing page. Your own law says data monetization should be visible, not hidden. Done right, this becomes both SEO capture and enterprise sales support.

**25. HC-W3-06 — Local Pack Conquest Program**
Run a real, truth-first local pack strategy: real entity completeness, real location/service-area accuracy, real local proof, real response quality, real review acquisition, and market-specific landing pages that connect to the actual business or claimant. No fake offices, no fake profile spam, no fake review games.

**26. HC-W3-07 — Directory ↔ App Feedback Loop**
Use the directory for public authority and the app for operational gravity. Every public page should create reasons to log in or install, and every app event should create proof, freshness, or public authority signals that can flow back into directory pages. That is directly consistent with your operating law.

**27. HC-W3-08 — Competitor Absorption Page System**
Create pages and flows that absorb competitor use cases: available now, instant request, forms hub, broker workflow, trust on results, route intelligence, marketplace density, and self-serve ad buying. Your prompt literally frames this as competitor absorption, so it belongs in the monopoly wave, not the someday pile.

## HARD RULES I WOULD NOT LEAVE OUT

- Do not let important pages stay orphaned or JS-hidden. Google still needs crawlable anchor links and site architecture it can actually traverse.
- Do not put structured data on pages where the content is thin, hidden, stale, or not visibly supported. That is exactly how rich result eligibility gets weakened.
- Do not use self-serving review stars on your own local business / organization pages. Use trust report cards, proof packets, and visible verification instead.
- Do not treat city pages as mere SEO placeholders. Each city page should have local proof, related corridors, nearby markets, regulations, tools, claim CTA, sponsor CTA, and live-ish but truthful activity. That is how you turn a city page into a market takeover page.
- Do not scale weak data sources. Your advisor dump already shows SEO-critical objects and event tables that need hardening, including `v_hreflang_sets`, `v_page_cannibalization_score`, `seo_schema_templates`, `seo_scorecards`, `seo_taxonomies`, `behavioral_events`, `tool_cta_clicks`, and `hc_ad_impressions`. Fix those before you trust the reporting they produce.
